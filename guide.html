📋 SafeTrack School Guide

Everything your school needs to know about SafeTrack — how it works, how staff use it, how parents register and use it, and everything in between. Simple. Clear. Step by step.

🛡️ What is SafeTrack?

SafeTrack is a school safety system built specifically for Nigerian primary and secondary schools. It automatically notifies parents the moment their child arrives or leaves school via SMS text message and email.

Every student gets a unique QR code printed on their ID card. When gate staff scan the card at the school gate using any smartphone, parents instantly receive a notification with the exact time.

SafeTrack also includes a secure Pickup PIN system — ensuring no child can ever be collected by an unauthorized person.

💡 How parents get notified

When a student arrives at school, their parent immediately receives an SMS saying:

"Amaka Okonkwo has arrived at Greenfield Academy at 7:52 AM. Powered by SafeTrack"

And when they leave:

"Amaka Okonkwo has departed Greenfield Academy at 2:30 PM. Powered by SafeTrack"

📱 How Parents Register Their Child

Parents register their children themselves directly on the SafeTrack app. It is simple and takes less than 5 minutes. No paperwork. No queues.

1

Open the SafeTrack Parent App

The parent opens their phone browser and goes to the SafeTrack parent portal. They tap "Register My Child" on the login screen.

2

Enter child's details

The parent fills in:
• Child's full name
• Child's class (e.g. JSS 2A)
• Select the school from the list

3

Take child's photo

The parent takes a clear passport photo of their child using their phone camera. This photo will appear on the printed ID card.

4

Enter parent contact details

The parent enters their:
• Phone number (for SMS notifications)
• WhatsApp number
• Email address (optional)

5

Confirm and submit

The parent reviews all the details and taps "Confirm and Register". Registration is automatic — no waiting for approval.

6

Screenshot the QR code

After registration, a unique QR code is generated for the child. The parent must screenshot this QR code and save it. They will need it to login to the app in future.

⚠️ Important for parents

The QR code shown after registration must be screenshotted and saved immediately. This QR code along with the parent's phone number is what they use to login to the SafeTrack parent app every time.

🪪 What happens after registration?

Once a parent registers their child, SafeTrack prints a professional plastic ID card with the child's photo and QR code. The ID card is delivered to the school within 48 hours. The school gives it to the student. From that day, the student scans the card at the gate every morning and afternoon.

👨‍💼 How School Staff Use SafeTrack

Gate staff only need one smartphone to run SafeTrack. No special equipment. No complicated training. Just open the app and scan.

1

Open the Gate App

Every morning, the gate staff opens the SafeTrack Gate app on their phone. The app is already installed — they just tap the icon on their home screen.

2

Tap "Activate Voice"

There is an orange banner at the top of the gate app. Staff tap it once to activate the voice system. The app will say "SafeTrack is ready! Have a wonderful day!"

3

Select Arrival mode in the morning

Tap the green "Arrival" button. This tells the system that students are arriving. Keep it on Arrival all morning.

4

Scan each student's ID card

Point the phone camera at the QR code on the student's ID card. The system will:
• Beep to confirm the scan
• Say "Welcome to school Amaka! Have a super day!"
• Show the student's name and arrival time on screen
• Automatically send SMS to the parent

5

Switch to Departure in the afternoon

When students are leaving, tap the red "Departure" button. Scan each student as they leave. The app will say "Bye bye Amaka! See you tomorrow!" and parents are notified automatically.

6

Verify Pickup PIN when needed

When someone comes to pick up a child, tap the "Verify PIN" tab at the top. Enter the 6-digit PIN the parent gave them. The system will show the child's name and the authorized pickup person's name.

⚠️ Critical Security Rule for Staff

When verifying a pickup PIN — always ask the pickup person "What is your name?" BEFORE looking at the screen. The name they say must match the name shown on screen. If the names do not match — do NOT release the child. Call the parent immediately.

🔒 What if a student tries to scan twice?

SafeTrack is smart. If a student has already been scanned as arrived and tries to scan for arrival again — the system blocks it and shows a warning. A student must depart before they can arrive again. This prevents any mistakes or fraud.

👨‍👩‍👧 How Parents Use SafeTrack Daily

📱

Install on Phone

Parents open their phone browser, go to the SafeTrack parent link, and tap "Add to Home Screen". SafeTrack appears on their phone like a real app.

🔑

Login Anytime

Parents login with their phone number and their child's QR code. They can check attendance history anytime — morning, afternoon or night.

📊

View Attendance History

Parents see every arrival and departure with the exact date and time. Full history is always available — not just today.

✅

Check Registration Status

Parents can see if their child's account is Active or Inactive. An active account means everything is working normally.

1

How to generate a Pickup PIN

When a parent needs to send someone else — uncle, aunty, driver — to pick up their child, they open the SafeTrack app and tap "Generate Pickup PIN".

2

Enter the pickup person's name

The parent types the full name of the person coming to pick up the child — e.g. Uncle Emeka Okafor. Then taps "Generate PIN".

3

Share the PIN

A 6-digit PIN appears on screen — valid for 30 minutes only. The parent sends this PIN to the pickup person via WhatsApp or phone call.

4

Pickup person presents PIN at gate

The pickup person tells the gate staff their PIN. Staff enter it into SafeTrack. The system shows the child's name and the authorized person's name. Staff confirm the name matches before releasing the child.

🖥️ How School Admin Uses SafeTrack

1

Login to School Dashboard

The school admin logs in with a unique username and password provided by SafeTrack during onboarding. Only the school admin has access to this dashboard.

2

View all registered students

The dashboard shows all students registered under the school — their names, classes, parent contacts and QR codes. The school can only see their own students.

3

View today's attendance

The dashboard shows every student who has arrived or departed today — with exact times. This updates in real time as students are scanned at the gate.

4

Filter attendance by date

Use the date filter to view attendance for any previous date. See who came on Monday, who was absent on Tuesday — complete historical records.

5

Deactivate or reactivate a student

If a student leaves the school, the admin can deactivate their account. Their QR code immediately stops working at the gate. If the student returns, the account can be reactivated instantly.

🔒 Three Layers of Security

SafeTrack is the only school safety system in Nigeria with three independent security layers protecting every child. Even if one layer fails — the other two still protect the child.

🪪

Layer 1 — QR Code

Every student has a unique QR code that cannot be copied or duplicated. Lost cards are deactivated immediately.

⏱️

Layer 2 — Time PIN

Pickup PINs expire after 30 minutes and can only be used once. Even if stolen — they become useless quickly.

👤

Layer 3 — Name Check

Staff ask the pickup person their name before checking the system. Name must match — no match means no release.

📱 Parent Notifications

Every time a student is scanned at the gate, their parent receives an automatic notification. No delay. No manual sending. It happens instantly.

📲

SMS Notification

Every parent receives an SMS text message the moment their child arrives or departs. Works on all Nigerian networks — MTN, Airtel, Glo, 9mobile.

📧

Email Notification

Parents who provide an email address also receive an email notification with full details — student name, school, time and date.
